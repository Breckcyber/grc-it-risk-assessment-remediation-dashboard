# IT Risk & GRC Home Lab

## Risk Assessment, Control Mapping & Remediation Dashboard

This project is a practical GRC home lab that simulates how a junior IT Risk or GRC Analyst would support an organization in identifying cybersecurity risks, documenting controls, tracking remediation, and reporting risk status to management.

The project is based on a fictional Canadian healthcare organization, **MapleCare Health Services**, which handles patient records, employee information, billing data, and internal business documents. The goal is to assess key IT and cybersecurity risks, connect them to business impact, map them to recognized frameworks, and prepare audit-ready documentation.

---

## Why I Built This Project

I built this project to show practical GRC skills beyond theory and course learning. Many GRC roles require the ability to assess risk, document controls, collect evidence, track remediation, and communicate risk clearly to business stakeholders.

This home lab demonstrates how I would approach that work in a real organization.

---

## Business Scenario

MapleCare Health Services is a fictional healthcare organization in Canada with approximately 150 employees. The organization uses Microsoft 365, SharePoint, a patient records system, an HR system, staff endpoints, and a third-party billing vendor portal.

Because the organization handles sensitive patient, employee, and billing information, leadership needs a clearer view of its IT risks, existing controls, control gaps, and remediation priorities.

---

## My Role

In this project, I acted as a **Junior IT Risk & GRC Analyst** responsible for supporting the risk assessment process.

My responsibilities included:

* Understanding the business environment
* Identifying key IT systems and business processes
* Documenting cybersecurity, vendor, access control, backup, and operational risks
* Scoring risks using impact and likelihood
* Identifying existing controls and control gaps
* Mapping risks to NIST CSF 2.0, NIST SP 800-53, and ISO/IEC 27001:2022
* Defining audit evidence requirements
* Tracking remediation actions, owners, due dates, and status
* Preparing risk reporting for management review

## Key Risk Areas Covered

The project focuses on realistic IT and cybersecurity risks, including:

* MFA not enforced for all users
* Former employees retaining access
* Unauthorized access to patient records
* Vendor security evidence not reviewed
* Backups not tested regularly
* Outdated incident response process
* Incomplete security awareness training
* Broad SharePoint permissions
* Inconsistent patch management tracking
* Sensitive data not classified
* Admin accounts not reviewed separately
* Lack of centralized risk documentation
* Audit evidence not consistently collected
